Physics Principles

1. Universal Gravitation

The motion of planets is governed by Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation, providing the necessary centripetal force for orbit.

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

2. Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

Planets follow elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus. The square of the orbital period ($T$) is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis ($a$).

$$T^2 = \left( \frac{4\pi^2}{GM} \right) a^3$$

This simulation solves Kepler's Equation numerically using the Newton-Raphson method to accurately position objects over time:

$$M = E - e \sin(E)$$

3. Astronomical Units (AU)

Distances in this simulation are measured in Astronomical Units (AU), where 1 AU is the average distance from the Earth to the Sun ($\approx 1.496 \times 10^{11}$ m). Time is measured in Earth Years.
