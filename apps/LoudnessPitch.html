Physics Principles

A sound wave is a longitudinal wave that propagates through a medium (like air) via pressure variations. A microphone converts these pressure changes into electrical signals, which we plot on a Displacement-Time Graph.

1. Amplitude (A)

Amplitude corresponds to the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position. Physically, a larger amplitude means greater pressure variation, resulting in a louder sound. In the graph, this stretches the wave vertically.

2. Frequency (f)

Frequency is the number of complete cycles per second, measured in Hertz (Hz). Physically, a higher frequency means the particles vibrate faster, resulting in a higher pitch. In the graph, a higher frequency compresses the wave horizontally (shorter period).

3. Quality (Timbre)

The quality of a sound enables us to distinguish between different musical instruments playing the same note at the same loudness. It is determined by the waveform.
