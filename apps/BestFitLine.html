Mathematical Principles

Linear regression finds the straight line that best fits a set of data points by minimizing the sum of the squares of the vertical residuals.

$$y = mx + c$$

The coefficient of determination, $R^2$, indicates how well the regression line approximates the real data points. A value of $1$ means a perfect fit.

$$R^2 = 1 - \frac{\sum (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2}{\sum (y_i - \bar{y})^2}$$
