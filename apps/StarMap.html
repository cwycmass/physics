Celestial Motions

1. Daily Motion (Diurnal Motion)

Every night, celestial objects (stars, planets, the Moon, and the Sun) appear to rise in the East and set in the West. This apparent motion is caused by the Earth rotating on its axis from West to East.

2. Stars vs. Planets

"Stars" maintain fixed positions relative to one another, forming the constellations we see on this map. "Planets" (from the Greek word for "wanderers") slowly change their positions against this background of fixed stars as they orbit the Sun.

3. Prograde & Retrograde Motion

Normally, planets move eastward relative to the stars (Prograde motion). However, occasionally a planet appears to temporarily reverse direction and move westward (Retrograde motion). This is an optical illusion that occurs when a faster-moving Earth overtakes an outer planet, or an inner planet overtakes the Earth.
